# GK Repair System: User Manual

Welcome to the **GK Repair System**! This manual covers the core workflows for both **Shop Owners** and **Shop Staff (Technicians)**.

---

## 1. Shop Owner Guide

As the **Shop Owner**, you are the administrator of the system. You have full access to shop configurations, staff management, financials, and advanced business reports.

### 1.1 First-Time Setup (Shop Registration)
1. Navigate to the registration page.
2. Enter your name, email, password, and the initial details of your shop: **Shop Name**, **Shop Address**, and **Shop Phone**.
3. Submit the form to register. 
   - *Note: The first registered user for any shop is automatically designated as the **Owner**.*
4. Upon successful registration, you will be redirected to the **Dashboard**.

### 1.2 Dashboard Overview
- **KPI Indicators**: Look at the top metrics cards to monitor:
  - **New Repairs Today**: Total tickets opened today.
  - **Delivered Today**: Total tickets completed and closed today.
  - **Revenue Collected Today**: Sum of advance payments collected today.
  - **Pending Deliveries**: Total tickets in the `ready` state awaiting pickup.
  - **Total Outstanding Balance**: Monies remaining to be collected upon customer delivery.
- **Analytics Charts**:
  - **Monthly Revenue**: A bar chart visualizing revenue trends and ticket counts over the last 6 months.
  - **Status Distribution**: A pie chart showing the count of tickets in `pending`, `repairing`, `ready`, `delivered`, and `cancelled` states.
  - **Top Device Brands**: A horizontal bar chart displaying the top 5 brands registered for repair.
- **Recent Activity**: Displays the 5 most recently created repair tickets with quick-access status selectors.

### 1.3 Managing Staff (Technicians)
1. Go to the **Settings** page and select the **Staff Management** tab.
2. To add a new technician, click **Recruit Staff Member**.
3. Fill in the staff member's **Name**, **Email**, and **Password**. 
4. Click **Create Staff Profile**. The system automatically generates a unique sequential staff ID (e.g. `GK001`, `GK002`, `GK003`...).
5. In the staff directory table, you can:
   - **Toggle Status**: Temporarily deactivate or reactivate a staff member's access.
   - **Trigger Password Reset**: Request a password update code or link for that technician.

### 1.4 General Store Settings
1. Navigate to **Settings** and select **Shop Profile**.
2. **Shop Logo**: Upload or update the store logo. This logo is embedded as vector-scaled headers on all customer PDF receipts.
3. **Store Information**: Edit the address, phone number, or store name.

### 1.5 Reports & Advanced Analytics
1. Navigate to **Reports** (accessible only to Owners).
2. **Date Range Filters**: Define starting and ending dates to pull historical data.
3. **Repairs Logs**: Review detailed repair tickets. Click **Download CSV** to export the structured log data for offline bookkeeping.
4. **Staff Performance Tracker**: View metrics for each technician, including:
   - Total tickets assigned.
   - Total tickets successfully delivered.
   - **Average Turnaround**: Average duration (in days) to complete a repair.
   - **Estimate Revenue**: Total financial volume generated by that staff member.
5. **Aging Repair Board**: View tickets sorting active issues by their age in days. Tickets are color-coded:
   - `0 - 3 days`: Green (Normal progress).
   - `4 - 7 days`: Amber (Warning: review progress).
   - `8+ days`: Red (Critical delay: immediate intervention needed).
   - *Action: Reassign delayed tickets to another technician directly via the dropdown menu.*

## 2. Shop Staff (Technician) Guide

As a **Staff Member (Technician)**, your view is streamlined to help you focus on your assigned queue and record repair work.

### 2.1 Accessing the System
1. Use the login credentials provided by your Shop Owner.
2. Once logged in, your **Dashboard** will filter statistics and active queues to display **only tickets assigned to you**.

### 2.2 Registering Customers & Devices
1. Select **New Repair** from the navigation sidebar.
2. **Step 1: Customer Info**:
   - Search for an existing customer by name or phone.
   - If they are a new customer, fill out the inline **Register Customer** form (Name, Phone, Address, and an optional camera snapshot of the customer).
3. **Step 2: Device Details**:
   - Enter the device Brand, Model, IMEI (optional), and Problem Description.
   - Select the device's current physical condition quality (Good, Fair, Poor, Damaged).
   - Provide a description of any existing physical damage (scratches, dents).
4. **Step 3: Hardware Snapshots**:
   - Capture or upload a **Front Photo** and **Back Photo** of the device to verify condition upon entry.
5. **Step 4: Financial Estimates & Assignment**:
   - Input the **Total Cost Estimate** and any **Advance Payment** made by the customer.
   - Select the **Expected Delivery Date** and assign the ticket (defaults to yourself).
6. Submit the form. The system will save the record and print a success message showing the assigned job number (format: `GK-YYYYMMDD-XXX`).

### 2.3 Performing Repairs & Updating Status
1. Navigate to **Repairs** to see your active ticket list.
2. Click on a repair to open the **Repair Details** page.
3. **Timeline Log**: View the status history and comments.
4. **Update Status**: 
   - Click the **Update Status** button.
   - Select the new status:
     - `pending`: Device awaiting diagnosis.
     - `repairing`: Diagnostics/repairs are active.
     - `ready`: Device is fully repaired and awaiting pickup.
     - `cancelled`: Customer rejected estimate or device is unfixable.
   - Add status notes explaining the work done.
   - *Note: The system automatically logs the date, state transition, notes, and your user ID to the permanent audit timeline.*

### 2.4 Handoff & Delivery Closure (Delivered)
When a customer arrives to collect their device, complete the handoff:
1. Open the repair ticket and verify that the status is set to `ready`.
2. Click **Deliver Device**.
3. **Handoff Checklist**:
   - Enter the name and phone number of the person collecting the device (if not the original customer).
   - **Signature Canvas**: Ask the receiver to sign directly on the digital signature pad.
   - **Handoff Photo**: Capture a webcam photo of the receiver with the repaired device.
4. Click **Complete Delivery**.
5. Once completed:
   - The status is updated to `delivered`.
   - Repaired device photos, signatures, and receiver details are permanently archived.
   - You can click **Print Receipt** or **Preview Receipt** to display the vector PDF receipt, complete with shop logo, financials, problem descriptions, and signature layout.
